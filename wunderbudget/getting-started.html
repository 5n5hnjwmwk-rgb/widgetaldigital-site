wunderBudget
Personal budgeting, powered by your bank.

Getting Started

Up and running in under 5 minutes.

1

Connect your bank

Tap the Accounts tab, then Connect a bank account. Search for your bank and sign in securely through Plaid — wunderBudget never sees your bank credentials.

✓ Most major US banks are supported including Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, and thousands of credit unions.
2

Sync your transactions

Tap Sync all accounts on the Accounts tab. wunderBudget will import up to 90 days of transaction history and automatically categorize everything using AI. This takes about 3–5 minutes on first sync.

✓ You'll see a live progress bar as your transactions import and get categorized.
3

Review low-confidence transactions

After syncing, an orange badge may appear on the Transactions tab. These are transactions the AI wasn't sure about. Swipe right to confirm the suggested category, or tap the tag icon to pick a different one.

✓ Each review you complete teaches the app — next sync those merchants get categorized automatically.
4

Set up your budget

Tap the Budgets tab. Navigate to the current month and tap any category to set a monthly limit. The donut chart updates live as you enter amounts so you can see your total budget take shape.

✓ Only enter limits for categories you actively want to track — leave the rest blank.
5

Clone to next month

Once your budget looks right, tap the copy icon (top right of the Budgets tab) to clone your budget to the next month. Adjust anything that changes — like a different tax payment or irregular expense — and you're set.

✓ Cloning takes seconds and keeps your budget setup consistent month to month.

After your transactions are imported, wunderBudget sends them in batches to Claude (an AI by Anthropic) along with your category list. Claude reads the merchant name, amount, and transaction type to assign the most likely category.

High-confidence assignments (85%+) are applied automatically and a merchant rule is saved so future transactions from the same merchant are categorized instantly without AI. Low-confidence items are flagged for your review.

A few types of transactions are genuinely ambiguous — paper checks, obscure merchant names, or transactions where the context isn't clear. These are left as "Unknown" and appear in the review queue for you to categorize manually.

Once you assign a category, a merchant rule is created and that merchant will be recognized automatically on future syncs.

Sync whenever you want to see your latest transactions — daily, weekly, or whenever you're checking your budget. wunderBudget only fetches what's new since your last sync, so it's fast after the first time.

Note: wunderBudget relies on Plaid to fetch transaction data. Plaid typically refreshes bank data every few hours, so very recent transactions may take a short time to appear.

Merchant rules are shortcuts that remember how you've categorized a merchant before. When a transaction comes in from a known merchant, wunderBudget applies the saved category instantly — no AI needed.

Rules are created automatically when the AI assigns a high-confidence category, or when you confirm a category during review. You can view and edit all rules in Settings → Merchant Rules. Changing a rule gives you the option to apply the new category to all matching transactions at once.
